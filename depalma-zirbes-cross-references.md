# Bruce DePalma and Lloyd Zirbes — Cross-References

Two independent researchers, both active in the late 1980s, each claiming to have demonstrated that the laws of energy conservation are incomplete — through different mechanisms. DePalma via the N-Machine (rotational inertia), Zirbes via his Theory of Falling Bodies (composition-dependent gravity). Both appeared on the same KPFK Los Angeles radio program hosted by Roy Tuckman, weeks apart in early 1988.

## 1. Jeffrey Harrison Introduces Zirbes by Referencing DePalma

**Source:** `transcripts/CD1_01.txt`, lines 1–14 (Zirbes radio interview, April 21, 1988)

Jeffrey Harrison, the KPFK host, introduces Zirbes to his audience by situating him relative to DePalma and Newman — researchers his listeners already knew:

> I looked at the book. This is about 300 page book and I do not present myself as a science person with a real heavy scientific background or scientific mind and certainly not a physicist, but this stuff seemed acceptable as a proposition that it is real, sort of look real to me and **this is beyond De Palma and beyond Newman, although in definitely in concert with him** seems to be working on the same stuff they are, **but he's been doing it longer** and he's come up not only with a generator, claims a generator that generate 300% efficiency, which of course they have running in India now and patented there, 300%, but that's only one of his claims. He claims an anti gravity generator, which is we're talking about a whole different ball of wax

Harrison positions Zirbes as going *further* than DePalma — not just free energy but gravity manipulation — while noting that all three are "in concert" working on the same underlying physics.

## 2. Zirbes Names DePalma as a Contemporary Who Proved Conservation Laws Incorrect

**Source:** `transcripts/CD2_01.txt`, lines 68–87 (Zirbes radio interview, April 21, 1988)

After discussing his 300% efficient gravity generator, Zirbes explicitly names DePalma:

> You have developed a generator that is 300% efficient, one megawatt turns the generator and produces three megawatts power. This is true, yes. Now modern physicists are again going to come up and say a perpetual motion, perpetual motion. No way impossible. Yes, it breaks the laws of energy conservation. And of course many people have proved that the laws of energy conservation are incorrect. **There are people right now in California, Dr. Bruce DePalma, has clearly proved it. Joe Newman has proved it. Along with a hundred other people, have proved that the energy conservation laws are incorrect, as I also have proved it.** Now they have proved that, but they have not developed this other technology.

Zirbes then draws a critical distinction — DePalma and Newman proved conservation is wrong via electromagnetic devices, but they haven't developed what Zirbes considers the deeper technology based on gravity itself:

> You see, these people are still functioning on the basic laws of physics, and until they back off and take a look at existing laws of physics, and take a look at — or do their own test on falling bodies — there, there's no way they're gonna get over this hump they're on.

## 3. DePalma Discusses Zirbes at Length — Personal Contact, Assessment, and Advice

**Source:** DePalma KPFK/PBS Part 2 transcript ([THcnZ5vqPRc](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=THcnZ5vqPRc), 1988)

In a sustained exchange during his own KPFK appearance, DePalma discusses Zirbes in three distinct passages. The host (Roy Tuckman) groups DePalma, Newman, and Zirbes together; DePalma responds with a nuanced, first-person assessment.

### 3a. The Host Groups All Three as "Crackpots" in the Eyes of Mainstream Science

> I want to say in the interest of fairness at this time that in the united states and although it sounds like that the old familiar story — you will be snubbed now but there'll be a movie about you in 50 or 100 years — that regular scientists, many regular scientists in the united states consider **Bruce DePalma and people as Joseph Newman and Lloyd Zirbes as either crackpots, liars, a con man** and I have been accused of programming bordering on criminality by having Bruce DePalma here not only once but repetitively, and so you are to be warned about that.

### 3b. DePalma Confirms Personal Contact — Urged Zirbes to Publish

DePalma reveals he spoke directly with Zirbes by phone and exchanged letters:

> Have you ever talked to him? **I have talked to him** and I have talked to him and I said my feeling — you know when people talk to me about free energy and things like that I said look, everything you say to me is going to be public knowledge. If you're going to keep secrets don't talk to me and that's my attitude from the very beginning. I have told everyone about everything I've done. **Now I spoke to Lloyd Zirbes about this and I said look Lloyd, if you want to have support to build your gravity generator, write a report, circulate it, let people know what you have.** Then somebody will come up and try to help you. If it really works the first guy that gets one is going to steal it and copy it so there's no possibility of keeping it a secret or anything, so don't — why don't you just give in to that right away.

### 3c. DePalma's Scientific Assessment — "Interesting Effects in Gravitation"

DePalma then gives his most considered evaluation — acknowledging Zirbes may have found something real in gravitation, while being candid about the limits of what he can assess without more data:

> I may not be as far out as Lloyd in some ways or maybe more far out than others. **I do think he has discovered some interesting effects in gravitation which have to be looked into** and whether or not they will lead to the anti-gravity principle that he says he has, I don't know because he won't tell me. And after I spoke to him on the phone I said make it public, I'll help you make it public and everything — **he said he would think about it and then he wrote me back and said that he didn't know whether he wanted to release it because it might be misinterpreted** or something like that.

### 3d. DePalma's Broader Assessment of Zirbes' Physics

Earlier in the same exchange, DePalma comments on Zirbes' theoretical approach:

> He thinks he has some new ideas. Yes, he's got to come up with a few different ways of understanding them. Now whether or not this will result in a spaceship or something like that has yet to be shown. You see, **it's a lot of hints are made in his work about what he would do with his gravity generator but never is it described.** So until I would see a description of some sort I couldn't tell.

And on the question of whether Zirbes is "looked upon like a nut":

> The only way that you avoid being looked at as a nut so to speak is if you make everything you know available and so people can look over your experiments — they never care too much about your theories.

## Significance for This Repository

These cross-references establish that Lloyd Zirbes was **known to and taken seriously by Bruce DePalma** — one of the most prominent alternative physics researchers of the 1980s. DePalma's assessment ("discovered some interesting effects in gravitation which have to be looked into") is notable for its measured tone: neither dismissive nor uncritically endorsing. The phone call and letter exchange confirm direct personal contact between the two researchers.

The KPFK radio program (hosted by Roy Tuckman) served as a hub connecting these researchers to each other and to the public. Both DePalma and Zirbes appeared on the same program in early 1988, establishing that the Los Angeles alternative science community was aware of Zirbes' work alongside DePalma's and Newman's.
